Ruwanwelisaya is one of the most sacred and magnificent Buddhist stupas in Sri Lanka, located in the ancient city of Anuradhapura. It was built by King Dutugemunu in the 2nd century BCE to honor Lord Buddha and preserve his sacred relics. The stupa is believed to enshrine a large collection of Buddha relics, making it one of the most important pilgrimage sites for Buddhists around the world.

The stupa is renowned for its pure white dome, symbolizing purity and enlightenment. Surrounding the structure are rows of stone elephant statues, which represent strength, guardianship, and devotion. Ruwanwelisaya stands as a masterpiece of ancient Sri Lankan architecture and engineering, reflecting the advanced skills and deep religious faith of the time.

For centuries, the stupa has been a center of worship, meditation, and religious ceremonies. Thousands of devotees visit daily to offer flowers, light oil lamps, and chant pirith in a calm and spiritual atmosphere. Ruwanwelisaya is not only a religious monument but also a powerful symbol of Sri Lanka’s Buddhist identity, cultural heritage, and enduring devotion to the teachings of the Buddha.
