Sri Dalada Maligawa, also known as the Temple of the Sacred Tooth Relic, is one of the most sacred and important Buddhist sites in Sri Lanka. It is located in the historic city of Kandy and houses the Sacred Tooth Relic of Lord Buddha. This revered relic has been protected for centuries and is considered a symbol of sovereignty and spiritual authority in Sri Lanka. According to history, the Tooth Relic was brought to the island from India in the 4th century CE during the reign of King Kirthi Sri Meghavarna.

The present Temple of the Tooth was built within the royal palace complex of the Kandyan Kingdom and showcases beautiful traditional Kandyan architecture, including carved wooden pillars, stone walls, and golden decorations. The temple is surrounded by the serene Kandy Lake and mist-covered hills, creating a peaceful spiritual atmosphere.

Sri Dalada Maligawa is a major pilgrimage site visited by thousands of devotees daily who come to pay homage during the three daily poojas. The annual Esala Perahera, a grand cultural procession held in Kandy, honors the Sacred Tooth Relic and attracts visitors from around the world.
