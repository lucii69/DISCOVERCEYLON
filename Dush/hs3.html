Mihintale is one of the most important historical and religious sites in Sri Lanka, located near Anuradhapura. It is known as the birthplace of Buddhism in the island, where Arahat Mahinda met King Devanampiyatissa in the 3rd century BC. This historic meeting led to the introduction of Buddhism to Sri Lanka, shaping the country’s culture and heritage.

The site consists of several ancient monuments, including stupas, caves, monasteries, and stone steps carved into the mountain. The grand stairway with over 1,800 steps leads to the summit, offering a spiritual journey as well as stunning views of the surrounding landscape. Kantaka Chetiya and Ambasthala Dagoba are among the most significant structures.

Today, Mihintale remains a sacred pilgrimage site and an important archaeological location. It symbolizes peace, devotion, and the deep-rooted Buddhist tradition of Sri Lanka.
