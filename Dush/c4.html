Located in the heart of Kotahena, Colombo, St. Lucia’s Cathedral is the seat of the Archbishop and the oldest parish church in Sri Lanka. Its origins trace back to a humble wood-and-cadjan chapel built in 1760 during the Dutch era. Over nearly thirty years of construction, it was transformed into the grand structure seen today, reaching completion in 1902. The cathedral is an architectural marvel, drawing heavy inspiration from St. Peter’s Basilica in Rome. It features a majestic facade supported by Ionic columns and is topped by seven life-sized statues of saints. A massive dome dominates the skyline, while the interior is adorned with intricate stained glass, marble altars, and the famous "Anthony Thomas" bell, which weighs over 2,000 kilograms.

As a spiritual center, the cathedral houses the unique dark-skinned statue of "Our Lady of Kotahena." It remains a vital landmark of Sri Lankan Catholic heritage, hosting thousands of worshippers within its expansive nave.
