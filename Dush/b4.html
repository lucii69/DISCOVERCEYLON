Dambulla Cave Temple, also known as the Golden Temple of Dambulla, is one of the most important and well-preserved Buddhist sites in Sri Lanka. It is located in the Central Province and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The temple complex consists of five main caves carved into a massive rock, rising about 160 meters above the surrounding plains.

The history of Dambulla Cave Temple dates back to the 1st century BCE, when King Valagamba sought refuge in these caves during his exile. After regaining his throne, he transformed the caves into a magnificent rock temple as an act of gratitude. Over the centuries, several kings contributed to the expansion and decoration of the temple.

Inside the caves are more than 150 statues of Lord Buddha, along with statues of kings and deities. The walls and ceilings are beautifully adorned with ancient frescoes depicting scenes from the life of the Buddha and important events in Buddhist history. These paintings cover a vast area and are considered masterpieces of Sri Lankan art. Today, Dambulla Cave Temple is a major pilgrimage and tourist destination, admired for its spiritual significance, historical value, and artistic beauty.
