Horton Plains National Park is a protected highland area located in Sri Lanka’s central hill country. Known for its cool climate and wide open grasslands, it is one of the country’s most unique and scenic natural attractions.

The park is famous for the World’s End viewpoint, a dramatic cliff with a sheer drop of nearly 900 meters, offering breathtaking views, especially in the early morning. Visitors can also enjoy nature trails leading to Baker’s Falls, a beautiful waterfall surrounded by forest.

Horton Plains is rich in biodiversity and is home to many endemic plant and animal species. Its peaceful environment, misty landscapes, and well-marked hiking paths make it an ideal destination for hikers, nature lovers, and eco-tourism enthusiasts.
