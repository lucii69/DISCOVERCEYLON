Yapahuwa Rock Fortress is an ancient rock citadel located in northwestern Sri Lanka. It served as the capital of the country in the 13th century during the reign of King Bhuvanekabahu I. Built on a massive granite rock rising about 90 meters high, Yapahuwa functioned as a well-defended fortress to protect the kingdom from South Indian invasions.

The site is especially famous for its beautifully carved stone stairway, considered one of the finest examples of medieval Sri Lankan architecture. Yapahuwa also gained great religious importance as it once housed the Sacred Tooth Relic of the Buddha. Remains of walls, ramparts, and palace buildings reveal the strategic and architectural skill of the period.

Today, Yapahuwa stands as an important archaeological site, attracting visitors with its historical value, artistic carvings, and panoramic views. It reflects a significant chapter of Sri Lanka’s political and religious history.
