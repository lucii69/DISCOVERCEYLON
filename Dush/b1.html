Sri Maha Bodhi, located in the sacred city of Anuradhapura, Sri Lanka, is one of the most important and revered Buddhist landmarks in the world. It is a sacred fig tree grown from a sapling of the original Bodhi Tree in Bodh Gaya, India, under which Lord Buddha attained enlightenment. The sapling was brought to Sri Lanka in the 3rd century BCE by Sanghamitta Theri, the daughter of Emperor Ashoka, and planted in 288 BCE during the reign of King Devanampiya Tissa.

Sri Maha Bodhi is recognized as the oldest living tree in the world with a continuously recorded history. For over two thousand years, it has been carefully protected and worshipped by kings, monks, and devotees. The tree symbolizes enlightenment, wisdom, peace, and compassion, which are central teachings of Buddhism.

Today, Sri Maha Bodhi remains a major pilgrimage site, attracting thousands of local and foreign devotees who come to offer flowers, light oil lamps, and meditate in its peaceful surroundings. The sacred tree stands as a powerful symbol of Sri Lanka’s rich Buddhist heritage and spiritual devotion.
