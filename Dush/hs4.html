Aluvihare Rock Temple is an important historical and religious site located in Matale, Sri Lanka. It dates back to the 3rd century BC and is closely connected with the reign of King Valagamba. The temple is built around a large rock formation and consists of several caves decorated with paintings and Buddha statues.

Aluvihare holds great significance in Buddhist history as the place where the Tripitaka (Buddhist scriptures) was first written down on ola leaves. This historic event helped preserve the teachings of the Buddha for future generations during a time of political instability and famine.

Today, Aluvihare Rock Temple is a respected place of worship and a popular heritage site. It reflects Sri Lanka’s strong Buddhist traditions and stands as a symbol of religious dedication, learning, and cultural preservation.
