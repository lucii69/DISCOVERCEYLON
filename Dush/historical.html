Yapahuwa Rock Fortress


Yapahuwa was a medieval rock fortress and capital of Sri Lanka in the 13th century.
It is famous for its ornamental stone stairway and historical importance.

Mihintale


Mihintale is known as the birthplace of Buddhism in Sri Lanka.
It is the site where Arahat Mahinda met King Devanampiyatissa,
marking the introduction of Buddhism to the island.

Aluvihare Rock Temple


Aluvihare Rock Temple in Matale is historically important as the place
where the Tripitaka was first written down on ola leaves during the reign of King Walagamba.
