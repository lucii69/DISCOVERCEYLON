St. Anthony’s Shrine is one of the most popular and revered Catholic pilgrimage sites in Sri Lanka. It is located in Kochchikade, Colombo, and is dedicated to St. Anthony of Padua, who is known as the patron saint of lost things and miracles. The shrine is visited by thousands of devotees daily, including people of different religions, who come to seek blessings and offer prayers.

The shrine has a long history dating back to the 19th century and has grown in importance over the years due to the strong faith of its devotees. Many believe that prayers offered at St. Anthony’s Shrine are answered, especially during times of difficulty and personal hardship.

The church features simple yet graceful architecture, creating a peaceful and welcoming environment for worship. Special services and novenas are held every Tuesday, attracting large crowds of faithful worshippers. The feast of St. Anthony, celebrated in June, is marked with great devotion and religious ceremonies. St. Anthony’s Shrine stands as a powerful symbol of faith, hope, and unity, bringing together people from all backgrounds in prayer and devotion.
