St. Anne’s Shrine is one of the most important and historic Catholic pilgrimage sites in Sri Lanka. It is located in Talawila, near Chilaw, along the northwestern coast of the island. The shrine is dedicated to St. Anne, the mother of the Virgin Mary, and has been a place of deep faith and devotion for several centuries.

According to tradition, Portuguese missionaries introduced devotion to St. Anne in the 16th century, and the shrine gradually became a major center of Catholic worship. St. Anne is believed to be a powerful intercessor, especially for families, mothers, and those seeking blessings for children.

The shrine is especially famous for its annual feast held in July, which lasts for several days and attracts thousands of pilgrims from across the country. Devotees often walk long distances as acts of penance and faith. The church, located near the sea, provides a calm and spiritual atmosphere for prayer and reflection. St. Anne’s Shrine is not only a religious center but also a symbol of strong faith, hope, and tradition, bringing together people of different communities in devotion and unity.
